A cocktail shaker is a device used to mix beverages (usually alcoholic) by shaking. When ice is put in the shaker this allows for a quicker cooling of the drink before serving.

Japanese-style mixing glass. Much like the Shaker, the mixing glass is a glass or metal container used to quickly chill cocktail drinks, primarily by stirring with ice using a spoon and straining with a strainer.

A bar spoon is a long-handled spoon used in bartending for mixing and layering of both alcoholic and non-alcoholic mixed drinks. Its length ensures that it can reach the bottom of the tallest jug or tumbler to mix ingredients directly in the glass. A bar spoon holds about 5 millilitres of liquid or 2.5 ml of liquid.

Shop Now

A cocktail strainer is a metal bar accessory used to remove ice from a mixed drink as it is poured into the serving glass. A type of sieve, the strainer is placed over the mouth of the glass or shaker in which the beverage was prepared; small holes in the device allow only liquids to pass as the beverage is poured.

Cocktail Recipes

Gimlet
Ingredients
  • 2 1/2 ounces gin
  • 1/2 ounce fresh lime juice
  • 1/2 ounce simple syrup
  • Garnish: lime wheel
Steps
  1. Add all ingredients into a shaker with ice and shake.
  2. Strain into a chilled cocktail glass or an Old Fashioned glass filled with fresh ice.
  3. Garnish with a lime wheel.
  4. (You can substitute 1 oz lime cordial, such as Rose's lime juice, for both the lime juice and simple syrup.)

Margarita
Ingredients
  • 2 oz Blanco tequila
  • 0.75 oz Fresh lime juice
  • 1/2 oz Pierre Ferrand Dry Curacao
  • 1/2 oz Agave syrup
  • Lime Wheel
  • Garnish: Salt rim
Steps
  1. Rub the rim of a rocks glass with a lime wedge, dip in salt to coat and set aside.
  2. Add remaining ingredients and ice and shake until well-chilled.
  3. Strain into prepared glass over fresh ice.
  4. Garnish with a lime wheel.

Old Fashioned
Ingredients
  • 0.33 oz cane syrup
  • 1 dash Angostura Bitters
  • 1 dash Bittermens Mole Bitters
  • 2 oz Bourbon
  • Garnish: orange peel
  • Garnish: lemon peel
Steps
  1. Add the syrup, bitters and water into a rocks glass, and stir until sugar is nearly dissolved.
  2. Fill the glass with large ice cubes, add the bourbon, and gently stir to combine.
  3. Express the oil of an orange peel over the glass, then drop in.
  4. Garnish with a lemon peel.
